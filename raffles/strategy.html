Raffles Family Office — CRM Project & Beyond
Synthesised from Strategic Council deliberation (4/5 consensus), public intelligence on 20+ Raffles personnel, industry analysis, and Arthur's 18-year career mapping.

Core Thesis — Why You, Why Now

I am the operator who codes — the only candidate who has run a HKD 120M business, sat in KKR deal rooms, led operational transformations at Proudfoot, AND built the AI-driven technology that powered all of it. I learn fast, I build faster, and AI amplifies everything I do — turning one person into the output of a team.

vs Traditional Developer

"You understand the business"

Developer builds what told, you build what's needed. You've sat in investor/consultant/operator chairs, translate between RM needs and system needs. No requirements doc needed.

vs Management Consultant

"You ship working systems"

Consultants deliver decks, you deliver working software. Proudfoot trained methodology, STT Mall proved execution. No handoff risk.

vs Salesforce Partner

"You think beyond configuration"

Partner configures what exists, you see what's missing. Integration layer, custom AI, internal tooling. Evaluate vendors, not just implement. Understand build vs buy from running own business.

vs In-House Hire

"Zero ramp-up time on business context"

Junior hire takes 6-12 months, you bring Bloomberg/ANZ/KKR day one. Speak the language: deal flow, IC, AUM, mandates, suitability. Trilingual covering APAC.

vs Anyone Without AI-Augmented Methodology

"Fundamentally different speed"

Learn new domains fast, AI amplifies speed. One operator = small team output. Structured methodology, not just tools.

Conversation Flow — Steering the Interview

1. First 5 minutes — Establish Credibility Fast 0-5 min

Lead with: "18 years at intersection of finance and technology — Bloomberg, ANZ (Alibaba privatisation), KKR ($300M fund), own business (entire tech stack). I learn fast and AI-augmented methodology lets me move from problem to working solution faster than expected."

Goal: Within 60 seconds, not a typical developer.

2. Minutes 5-15 — Listen, Don't Pitch 5-15 min

Ask: "What's the core problem you're trying to solve with this CRM project?" Listen for: CRM vs broader tech/ops, building or buying, current systems, stakeholders.

Goal: Understand their framing before you respond.

3. Minutes 15-30 — Bridge Experience to Needs 15-30 min

Select stories based on what they said. Deal flow → KKR. Client engagement → STT Mall CRM. Process improvement → Proudfoot. Scale → STT Mall 10x.

Goal: Show, don't tell. Every story is a proof point.

4. Minutes 30-40 — Demonstrate Domain Depth 30-40 min

Entity modelling for family structures, multi-jurisdiction compliance, build-vs-buy framework, custodian integration. Vocabulary: "suitability assessment", "beneficial ownership", "mandate tracking."

Goal: You're not learning the domain — you already know it.

5. Minutes 40-50 — Plant the AI Seed (Only if Natural) 40-50 min

"One of the things that makes me effective is that I pick up new domains quickly — and I've developed an AI-augmented methodology..."

Goal: Don't force it. Only if there's a natural opening.

6. Last 10 minutes — Close with First 90 Days Last 10 min

"I've been thinking about first 90 days. My approach is listen-first: two weeks shadowing before recommending anything."

Goal: Leave them seeing you in the role.

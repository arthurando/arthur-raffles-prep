Hard Questions — Prepared Answers

Red = tough/adversarial. Blue = exploratory.

RED — TOUGH

Your CRM experience is e-commerce. What makes you think you can build for a family office?

Acknowledge gap, bridge it. 55K e-commerce subscribers and 200 UHNW families are different worlds in sensitivity, compliance, relationship depth. But underlying data architecture is structurally the same: hierarchical entities, multi-dimensional scoring, automated workflows, strict access controls. I've built that architecture.

Plus I sat in KKR deal rooms, ANZ coverage teams, Proudfoot transformations. I understand investment world from inside.

✓ KKR + ANZ + Proudfoot proof
RED — TOUGH

Walk me through handling a client with assets spanning four jurisdictions with conflicting data residency laws.

Requires collaboration with compliance and legal. Architecturally: jurisdiction-aware data partitioning with clear policies on cross-border aggregation. HK PDPO and Singapore PDPA have different consent models. I would define governance policy with compliance first, then implement technical architecture to enforce it.

✓ PDPO + PDPA awareness proof
RED — TOUGH

Our clients will not accept their data in any cloud AI system.

Privacy-first architecture. AI features for client-facing use: on-premise or private-cloud models. No third-party API calls. For internal productivity: anonymised/synthetic data. Start with rule-based automation, layer in private AI models as trust grows.

✓ Privacy-first proof
RED — TOUGH

What is your experience with SFC and MAS regulatory reporting?

Honest + homework. Haven't built for SFC/MAS specifically. Have worked within regulated institutions — ANZ, Bloomberg. Understand different licensing regimes, reporting cadences, data requirements. Critical CRM touchpoints: audit trails, AML/KYC capture, suitability tracking. I'd translate regulatory requirements into system specs — that translation layer is exactly where I operate best.

✓ ANZ + Bloomberg proof
RED — TOUGH

Why should we trust a self-taught developer with systems handling clients' most sensitive data?

Reframe to "rapid learner with engineering discipline." Three things:

(1) Core CRM should use proven auditable platforms, not me writing security-critical code solo.

(2) "Self-taught" means I learn fast by doing — chemical engineering to finance to production systems. 55K customer records, real transactions, 11 years.

(3) AI-augmented methodology with automated quality gates — TDD, security review, code review built in. "Imperial engineering degree gave analytical foundation; 11 years gave judgment; AI amplifies both."

✓ 11 years production + TDD proof
BLUE — EXPLORATORY

What happens when you leave? Can someone else maintain what you build?

"This is why I advocate proven platforms as foundation — Salesforce, DealCloud — with deep ecosystem. Custom work follows strict standards: TDD, documentation, standard frameworks. Goal: systems that outlast me."

✓ TDD + Standards proof
BLUE — EXPLORATORY

What CRM platform would you recommend?

"Depends on pain points. Relationship intelligence: DealCloud or 4Degrees. Enterprise customisation: Salesforce FSC. Lighter advisor workflow: Wealthbox or Redtail. But right answer requires understanding current systems, day-to-day struggles, 18-month roadmap. I'd spend first two weeks on proper technology assessment."

✓ Platform knowledge proof

Questions to Ask Raffles

Smart questions signal depth. Ask these to demonstrate domain understanding and gather intel.

Current Technology

What systems are you currently using for client relationship management? Looking to replace, augment, or integrate?

Why it works: Opens the door. Shows you're not assuming anything. Real constraints reveal alignment opportunity.
Pain Points

When a new RM joins, how long to get full context on a family's history and relationships?

Why it works: Onboarding speed is a proxy for data organization. Reveals information silos and knowledge gaps you can solve.
App Roadmap

Your RFO app is on its third upgrade — what has the journey taught you about what clients actually value?

Why it works: Specific. Shows you've done homework. Reveals product philosophy and user feedback patterns.
Compliance & Reporting

With Revo Digital now SFC-licensed, how does digital asset reporting integrate with broader client view?

Why it works: Deep domain knowledge. Signals you understand the ecosystem and asset class expansion challenge.
Scale & Jurisdiction

As you expand across markets, how do you manage compliance differences between HK and Singapore?

Why it works: Identifies the hard architectural problem. Shows you think in systems, not features.
Role Definition

What does success look like in 12 months? Technical architect, product owner, or hybrid operator?

Why it works: Clarifies scope and your role. Grounds the conversation in measurable outcomes.

First 90 Days Framework

Have this mental model ready. Adjust based on actual scope.

Phase 1 — Days 1-30
Listen & Map

  • Shadow every team member 2+ hours
  • Map current tech stack, data flows, integration points
  • Document pain points and workarounds
  • Understand compliance per jurisdiction
  • Review RFO app architecture and roadmap
  • Build vendor/partner relationship map

  • Identify 2-3 high-impact, low-risk improvements
  • Prototype solutions for most painful workflow
  • Present CRM strategy: build/buy/integrate recommendation

  • Begin custodian/bank integration assessment

Execute + Prove

  • Begin CRM platform config or procurement
  • Build first integration prototype (highest-value source)
  • Establish dev methodology and quality gates
  • Present 6-month roadmap to leadership
Deliverables

Working Quick Wins + Roadmap + Methodology

Landmines — What NOT to Say

These will undermine you. Know them. Avoid them.

⚠️

DO NOT: "I built a CRM"

You built a marketing automation and customer intelligence platform for e-commerce. Not a wealth management CRM. This category error will cost you.

Say instead: "Customer intelligence platform with entity modelling and segmentation — architectural patterns translate directly."
⚠️

DO NOT: Propose replacing existing stack

They have app on 3rd upgrade. They're invested. Suggest you'd blow it up and they hear "doesn't understand context."

Frame value as: "additive — extend capabilities, deepen integrations, build on what's working."
⚠️

DO NOT: Lead with AI before understanding problems

AI is a solution. Ask first. "Solution in search of a problem" risk is real with this demographic. They've seen the hype cycle.

Approach: "Understand the workflows first. Then recommend where AI genuinely reduces friction."
⚠️

DO NOT: Mention routing data through external LLM APIs

UHNW families won't tolerate third-party AI. On-premise/private-cloud only. Your AI methodology is for YOUR dev process, not theirs.

When asked about AI: "Any AI touching client data runs on-premise or in private cloud. Zero exposure to external APIs."
⚠️

DO NOT: Undersell regulatory complexity

Never say "just use Supabase" or "compliance is just another feature." Compliance is foundational, not bolt-on.

Use: "Requires close collaboration with compliance team. We build the system; they define the rules. Non-negotiable."
⚠️

DO NOT: Describe Claudia/Max by name

In family office context, "Claudia as Chief of Staff" and "Max the workhorse" sounds whimsical and diminishes credibility.

Say instead: "Structured AI-augmented development methodology with multi-perspective decision analysis and automated quality gates."

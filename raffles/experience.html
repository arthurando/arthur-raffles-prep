Four Stories

Building a CRM from Zero to 55K Subscribers

▲
Situation
STT Mall had no customer intelligence — transactions anonymous, repeat rates unknown, zero segmentation.
Task
Design CRM to segment, engage, retain 55,000+ subscribers across email, social, direct channels.
Action
Built segmentation on Supabase/PostgreSQL with Klaviyo integration. Behavioural scoring (purchase frequency, recency, basket). Automated engagement flows. RLS for team access. AI-augmented development methodology.
Result
55,497 active subscribers. Automated engagement drove retention. Full lifecycle. All by one person, AI-amplified.
Bridge to Raffles
Architecture pattern — hierarchical entity classification, behavioural scoring, automated engagement, access-controlled views — maps directly to family office. Scale different (55K consumers vs ~200 UHNW families) but data architecture structurally identical.
Honest Framing
Do NOT say "I built a CRM for wealth management." Say: "I built a customer intelligence platform for e-commerce. The entity modeling and segmentation patterns translate directly, though I fully appreciate that family office adds regulatory compliance, multi-jurisdiction data governance, and UHNW-sensitive information."
